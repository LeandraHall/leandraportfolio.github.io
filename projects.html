Space Cadet: The Return of the Moon - 2024

As part of my Education Technology class, I developed a video game using the coding language Scratch that would teach the player the lunar phases as well as the importance of the moon. The game is titled "Space Cadet: The Return of the Moon" and anyone over the age of 12 can play it.

The plot of the game follows a Space Cadet, the user, who was sent by Mission Control to collect pieces of the moon, which had been broken apart due to an asteroid, with the assistance of the Earth. The user would then retrieve each piece in a maze of space debris/asteroid belt while avoiding a rogue asteroid. Each piece would correspond to a phase of the moon which the Earth would explain what it is as the user collects it. If the user collects all four pieces of the moon without losing all three lives the user would win. If not then it was game over but they would have the option to try again.

For the video game, I first did research on the moon in relation to how it affects our tides, the Earthâ€™s orbit around the sun, and the negative impacts that would occur if the moon would disappear. I also researched the phases of the moon so I would know its technical names and how to depict them in the video game. Once that research finished, I began developing the game. I wrote, designed, drew, programmed, and tested the entire game myself.

Face Puzzle - 2023

As part of my Innovation, Art, and Technology class, I created a face puzzle out of wood by utilizing a lasercutter, wood staining, denim, paint markets, and acrylic paint.

To accomplish this piece, I took a photo of my face, superimposed a picture of a wolf, and created a new work based off the two pictures in Adobe Illustrator. For the linework, the red represents what would be cut by the laser while the blue represents what would be engraved into the wood. After the wood was cut, denim was also cut by the laser cutter and was then glued onto the wood. The wood was then stained and painted to my desire.

Gafas - 2023

As part of my Innovation, Art, and Technology class, I recreated my pair of glasses into a 3D printed model titled the Gafas or Glasses Projects.

I sculpted my glasses using Autodesk's Tinkercad software, uploaded the model into 3D-printing software, and then 3D printed it. After it was printed, I removed the support pieces for printing, sanded down the model, primed it, and then painted black. I completed this project myself under the guidance of my professor.

Heavenly Bodies - 2020-2021

In high school, I began developing a body of work titled Heavenly Bodies.

I wanted to depict personifications of significant figures within our solar system through reflections of the multitude of cultures we have here on Earth. For an entire school year, I designed each planet along with our sun and moon by researching heavily on various global ethnic indentities. Additionally, I experiemented with different drawing and lighting techniques with each piece. I submitted this body of work as my portfolio for the Advanced Placement 2-D Studio Art Exam.
